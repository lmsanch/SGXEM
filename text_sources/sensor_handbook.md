# Sensor Handbook — Acoustic & Thermal Detection Ranges

## Acoustic Detection Ranges by Vehicle Class

| Vehicle Class | Acoustic Detection Range | Frequency Band | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Wheeled APC | 800 m | 50–300 Hz | Low-frequency engine rumble; detectable at range in open terrain |
| Tracked IFV | 650 m | 80–500 Hz | Track clatter broadens signature; detectable despite lower speed |
| Light tactical vehicle | 400 m | 100–800 Hz | Smaller engine, shorter range; easily confused with civilian trucks |
| Heavy transport truck | 550 m | 40–250 Hz | Diesel signature dominant; low-frequency propagation |
| MRAP (mine-resistant) | 500 m | 60–400 Hz | V-hull engine mount creates distinctive low-harmonic pattern |
| Motorcycle recon unit | 250 m | 200–2000 Hz | High-frequency, short range; wind noise interference above 150 m |
| Amphibious assault vehicle | 700 m | 30–200 Hz | Water coupling extends low-frequency propagation when afloat |

## Thermal Detection Ranges by Vehicle Class

| Vehicle Class | Thermal Detection Range (FLIR) | IR Persistence after shutdown | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Wheeled APC | 3,200 m | 45 min | Large engine block; high contrast on desert terrain |
| Tracked IFV | 2,800 m | 60 min | Tracks retain heat; long persistence after engine off |
| Light tactical vehicle | 1,800 m | 20 min | Small engine cools quickly; low contrast at range |
| Heavy transport truck | 2,500 m | 35 min | Cargo area adds thermal mass |
| MRAP | 2,200 m | 40 min | V-hull retains engine heat |
| Motorcycle recon unit | 900 m | 10 min | Minimal thermal mass; easily lost in clutter |
| Amphibious assault vehicle | 3,000 m | 50 min | Water spray cooling offsets larger engine; variable |

## Cross-Reference Notes

- Acoustic and thermal ranges are measured under standard conditions (20°C ambient, flat terrain, no precipitation).
- Wheeled APC has the **longest acoustic detection range** among ground vehicles due to low-frequency propagation.
- Tracked IFV has the **longest thermal persistence** due to track heat retention.
- All ranges degrade 30–50% in urban environments due to multi-path reflections (acoustic) and building occlusion (thermal).
- Motorcycle recon units are the only class where acoustic detection exceeds thermal detection range under standard conditions.
